Four unequal power strokes, not four identical 2.5-bp steps?

The textbook burst is 4 × 2.5 bp. Where the unequal steps come from

Per-subunit axial height in the helical staircase (our ENM, apo→7JQQ). The 5th subunit (E, the anchor) doesn't translocate — it holds the regulatory contact. The four workers' descents (their tread heights) are the four (unequal) substeps.

What our data actually supports

Uneven, graded treads. The four worker heights differ, so their four descents differ — not four identical 2.5 bp. (The 8.3 Å is just the staircase span, max−min — a closure artifact, not a physical hinge.)

Multi-mode (33%). A rigid 5-fold screw gives equal steps; a soft multi-mode deformation gives unequal ones.

The model predicts it. P→H→P's own "position-dependent amplitude (0→35 Å)" is the statement that different subunits give different-sized strokes.

Separate, slow effect: as the head fills, internal pressure shrinks the overall step (10→8.9 bp, Liu 2014) — a modulation over thousands of bp, not the within-burst inequality.
