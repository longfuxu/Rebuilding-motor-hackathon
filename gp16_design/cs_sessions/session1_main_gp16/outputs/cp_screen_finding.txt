THE RULE (region-gated, not penalty-gated):
  C-domain 7/10 sites close | N-domain 1/7 | hinge 0/3.
  Closure vs raw cut_penalty is WEAK (point-biserial r=-0.34, p=0.20; Mann-Whitney p=0.10) -- the productive
  variable is WHICH FOLDING UNIT the cut lands in (C-domain), not the raw penalty. Notably the two LOWEST-penalty
  hinge sites (cp228 pen6, cp217 pen33) do NOT robustly close, while mid-penalty C-domain sites (cp285 pen61,
  cp297 pen60) do -- penalty alone is not predictive.

SWEET-SPOT vs cp233: cp234, cp236, cp285 MATCH cp233 (robust at all 3 inter-linkers); none clearly BEATS it.
cp233 sits in a contiguous C-domain closure window (230-236) PLUS a second independent C-domain site (cp285) ->
this is a SYSTEMATIC region-gated result, not a chance single hit. Best design remains cp233_int15_inter10.

FUNCTIONAL INTERPRETATION of the closing sites: all productive sites lie in the C-domain (229-330), which forms
the inter-subunit C-ring; cutting there places the new termini at the natural C-domain/C-domain packing interface,
where a short inter-subunit linker can bridge without threading wrong. Cutting in the N-domain (which forms a
separate inter-subunit N-ring) or the hinge severs the folding unit that determines subunit register, so the ring
either fails to close (N-dom) or closes with unstable partner assignment (hinge, Boltz-seed-variable).

ONE-LINE CONCLUSION: A whole-protein screen of 20 circular-permutation cut sites (penalty 6-198) shows ring
closure is gated by folding-unit region -- the C-domain is productive (7/10 close incl. a new robust site cp285),
the N-domain and hinge are not -- proving cp233 is a systematic, region-defined hit rather than a chance success.
