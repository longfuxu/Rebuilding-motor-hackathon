Understanding molecular motors by rebuilding them

North star: what are the design principles of a maximally powerful ring motor — and is a single covalent chain the answer, or the obstacle? We attack it by rebuilding: reconstruct oligomeric ring ATPases as single, genetically-addressable chains, predict their mechanics, and try to make them better than evolution's.

The answer, honestly: single-chain is not automatically the answer. It out-couples native for φ29 (an optimized circular permutation) but not for ClpX — so "maximally powerful" is a topological principle, and single-chain helps only when the topology is right. What we deliver is the closed loop: predict the build method, rank the coupling, and design incrementally stronger, single-molecule-testable motors.

Judging · never global confidence

A five-level metric hierarchy

We steer by interface geometry, never pTM/pLDDT — the highest-confidence RFdiffusion ring had the worst M2.

M1
A closed, in-order ring? — compact, sequential-consistent.
M2
Does each trans arginine finger reach the neighbour's ATP pocket? — <8 Å, sequential, handedness-robust. The catalytic-coupling soul.
M3
Can dsDNA thread the channel? — grounded from the gp16+DNA+pRNA complex. The stricter, function-level filter.
M4
Is the pRNA-binding surface preserved? — 29 grounded contacts.
M5
Is the ATP pocket intact? — Walker-A/B + the full catalytic set.

The surprise · and the honest boundary

A designed motor can out-couple native — but it's design-specific

gp16's single chain is more coordinated than native (Y129 is a 4.6× more central force hub — and the biochemistry independently shows Y129 is essential). ClpX does not generalize. So it is not a "covalency law" — nature's oligomer trades tighter coupling for self-assembly, evolvability, and the special-subunit regulation only an addressable single chain can dissect.

Honest boundaries

Limitations

  • Proxies rank, they don't give absolute force — single-molecule is ground truth.
  • Single-sequence prediction fails large rings (needs tiled MSA); pLDDT is a trap; covalent-ring M2 must be handedness-robust.
  • Apo folds are blind to channel fouling; the buildability rule rests on N≈3–4; the ClpX comparison is confounded; no wet-lab yet.

Every limitation points to the next step: computation issues a falsifiable prediction, single-molecule biophysics tests it.

How we used Claude

Claude Code orchestrated everything — driving GPU jobs (RFdiffusion, MD, NIM folding), running a 19-sub-agent scoring workflow (score → adversarially verify → synthesize), and coordinating a dozen parallel agents (design, coupling, literature, directed evolution, buildability, biochem calibration). Claude Science ran the systematic folding campaigns. The biggest leverage: parallel fan-out with adversarial verification — every headline number was independently re-checked, and the honest negatives (the ClpX result, the pLDDT trap) were surfaced, not hidden.
